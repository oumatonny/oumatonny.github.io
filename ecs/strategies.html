Strategies for Practice

Evidence-based approaches for quality early childhood education

Evidence-Based Teaching Strategies

Effective early childhood education relies on research-informed practices that support children's holistic development and learning.

Core Teaching Strategies

Play-Based Learning

Description: Learning through child-initiated and educator-supported play experiences.

Evidence: Research shows play enhances cognitive, social, emotional, and physical development.

Application:

  • Provide open-ended materials and resources
  • Create engaging learning environments
  • Observe and extend children's play
  • Balance child-led and adult-guided activities

Intentional Teaching

Description: Purposeful, thoughtful, and deliberate teaching actions.

Evidence: Intentional teaching maximizes learning opportunities and outcomes.

Application:

  • Plan learning experiences based on observations
  • Use scaffolding techniques
  • Ask open-ended questions
  • Model thinking and problem-solving

Collaborative Learning

Description: Learning through interaction and cooperation with peers.

Evidence: Peer interaction enhances social skills and cognitive development.

Application:

  • Facilitate group projects and activities
  • Encourage peer teaching and support
  • Create opportunities for shared problem-solving
  • Model cooperative behaviors

Learning Through Nature

Description: Outdoor and nature-based learning experiences.

Evidence: Nature play supports physical health, creativity, and environmental awareness.

Application:

  • Incorporate outdoor learning daily
  • Use natural materials in activities
  • Explore local environments
  • Foster environmental stewardship

Inclusive and Culturally Responsive Practices

Cultural Responsiveness

Recognize and value diverse cultural backgrounds, languages, and family structures.

  • Incorporate diverse perspectives in curriculum
  • Use multilingual resources and materials
  • Celebrate cultural events and traditions
  • Partner with families to understand cultural practices

Universal Design for Learning

Create flexible learning environments that accommodate all children's needs.

  • Provide multiple means of representation
  • Offer various ways to engage and participate
  • Support diverse expression and communication
  • Adapt materials and activities as needed

Trauma-Informed Practice

Understand and respond to the impact of trauma on children's development.

  • Create safe and predictable environments
  • Build trusting relationships
  • Use positive behavior support strategies
  • Collaborate with families and specialists

Strength-Based Approach

Focus on children's abilities, interests, and potential rather than deficits.

  • Identify and build on individual strengths
  • Use positive language and feedback
  • Set achievable goals with children
  • Celebrate progress and achievements

Assessment & Documentation Strategies

Learning Stories

Narrative documentation capturing children's learning moments and progress.

Portfolios

Collections of children's work showing development over time.

Observations

Systematic recording of children's behaviors, interactions, and learning.

Family Engagement Strategies

Communication
  • Regular updates through various channels
  • Two-way communication opportunities
  • Multilingual communication options
  • Accessible and welcoming environments
Participation
  • Family involvement in program planning
  • Volunteer and participation opportunities
  • Family events and celebrations
  • Home learning extension activities
Partnership
  • Shared decision-making processes
  • Respect for family knowledge and expertise
  • Collaborative goal-setting
  • Regular feedback and input opportunities
Support
  • Referrals to community services
  • Parent education workshops
  • Resource sharing and information
  • Emotional and practical support

Practical Application Examples

Scenario: Creating a construction play area

Strategy: Provide blocks, loose parts, and tools; observe children's interests; extend learning through questions and challenges.

Learning Outcomes: Problem-solving, spatial awareness, collaboration, mathematical concepts, creativity.

Scenario: Enhancing vocabulary during mealtimes

Strategy: Use descriptive language, ask open-ended questions, introduce new words, engage in conversations.

Learning Outcomes: Vocabulary expansion, communication skills, social interaction, cultural awareness.

Scenario: Creating a calm-down corner

Strategy: Designate quiet space with sensory tools, teach self-regulation strategies, model emotional awareness.

Learning Outcomes: Emotional literacy, self-regulation, coping skills, wellbeing.
