Social Policy & Australian Responses

Frameworks, policies, and evaluation systems shaping early childhood services

Policy Landscape

Australia has developed comprehensive policy frameworks to ensure quality, accessibility, and affordability of early childhood education and care services.

National Quality Framework (NQF)

Introduced in 2012, the NQF provides a national approach to regulation, assessment, and quality improvement for early childhood services.

Key Components: National Law, National Regulations, National Quality Standard, and Learning Frameworks

National Quality Standard (NQS)

The NQS sets a national benchmark for early childhood services, comprising seven quality areas:

Educational Program & Practice

Programs that enhance children's learning and development

Children's Health & Safety

Ensuring children's health, safety, and wellbeing

Physical Environment

Spaces that support learning and development

Staffing Arrangements

Qualified and experienced educators

Relationships with Children

Respectful and equitable relationships

Collaborative Partnerships

Partnerships with families and communities

Governance & Leadership

Effective leadership and management

National Learning Frameworks

Early Years Learning Framework (EYLF)

For children birth to 5 years

Five Learning Outcomes:
  1. Children have a strong sense of identity
  2. Children are connected with and contribute to their world
  3. Children have a strong sense of wellbeing
  4. Children are confident and involved learners
  5. Children are effective communicators

Framework for School Age Care

For children 5 to 12 years

Five Learning Outcomes:
  1. Children have a strong sense of identity
  2. Children are connected with and contribute to their world
  3. Children have a strong sense of wellbeing
  4. Children are confident and involved learners
  5. Children are effective communicators

Evaluation & Quality Assessment

Services are assessed against the NQS by regulatory authorities and receive one of five ratings:

  • Excellent: Demonstrates exceptional practice
  • Exceeding NQS: Exceeds the standard in all areas
  • Meeting NQS: Meets the national standard
  • Working Towards NQS: Developing quality practices
  • Significant Improvement Required: Does not meet requirements

All services must develop and implement a QIP that:

  • Identifies strengths and areas for improvement
  • Sets goals and strategies for quality enhancement
  • Involves families and educators in the process
  • Is reviewed and updated regularly

Regulatory authorities monitor services through:

  • Regular assessment and rating visits
  • Compliance monitoring and inspections
  • Investigation of complaints and incidents
  • Review of documentation and records
